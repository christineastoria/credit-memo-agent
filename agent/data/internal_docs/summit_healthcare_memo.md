# Summit Healthcare Partners — Annual Review Memo

**Analyst:** R. Patel | **Date:** December 3, 2024 | **Rating:** B+ (Stable)

## Business Overview
Summit Healthcare Partners operates a network of 45 outpatient surgery centers and 12 specialty clinics across the Southeast US. The company focuses on orthopedic, cardiovascular, and ophthalmology procedures. Founded in 2010, PE-backed since 2019. ~3,100 employees, HQ in Nashville, TN.

## Financial Summary (LTM Q3 2024)
- Revenue: $1.1B (up 9% YoY)
- EBITDA: $220M (margin: 20.0%)
- Total Debt: $750M (Term Loan B: $450M, HY Notes: $300M)
- Net Leverage: 3.2x (down from 3.6x at close)
- Interest Coverage: 2.8x
- Free Cash Flow: $78M
- Same-center revenue growth: 5.2%

## Key Strengths
- Favorable demographic trends: aging population drives orthopedic/cardio volume
- Outpatient shift from hospital settings continues — CMS reimbursement tailwinds
- Proven de novo center playbook (8 new centers opened since 2021, all profitable within 18 months)
- Physician alignment model creates sticky referral patterns

## Key Risks
- Reimbursement risk: Medicare/Medicaid represent 38% of payor mix — rate changes could impact margins
- Geographic concentration in Southeast US (FL, GA, TN, NC = 85% of revenue)
- Physician recruitment and retention increasingly competitive
- Leverage still elevated at 3.2x — limited cushion for operational miss
- Supplier concentration: top 3 implant manufacturers represent 60% of supply costs

## Capital Structure Notes
- Term Loan B: L+525, maturity 2028, current price ~98.5
- 8.25% Senior Notes: maturity 2027, callable at 104.125
- Revolver: $75M, undrawn
- Sponsor equity: ~$400M (2019 basis)

## Recommendation
Credit stable but leverage remains a concern. The business benefits from strong secular tailwinds in outpatient surgery. Main risk is a reimbursement shock or geographic concentration event (hurricane season exposure in FL/GA). Maintain current exposure. Review again at Q1 2025 earnings for progress on leverage reduction target of 3.0x.
